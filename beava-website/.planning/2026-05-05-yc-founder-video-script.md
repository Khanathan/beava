# YC Founder Video Script — Beava (Summer 2026)

> ~155 words. Paces to ~60 seconds at natural delivery speed.

---

## Script

I'm Hoang. I built feature stores at Faire, and at Fennel — the YC-backed real-time feature store Databricks acquired last year.

At Viggle, I tried to ship a simple real-time personalization feature — rank pages based on a user's last sixty seconds of clicks. I picked Quix Streams, the lightest option I knew. It still wasn't light enough. Kafka, glue scripts, things broke constantly. The aggregation should have been one Python file. There was no light path.

That's why I'm building Beava — a single-binary real-time feature server. Apache 2.0. Same shape as Polars, except it runs forever and serves features by key. Pip install, ten lines of Python, microsecond reads.

Tecton just got acquired. Chalk's expensive. Feast is offline-first. The bottom ninety percent of the market has nowhere to go. I want them to go to Beava.

I'm two weeks from public v0, looking for design partners — Series-A fintechs and ad-tech teams about to write two hundred lines of Redis Lua and don't want to.

Thanks.

---

## Delivery notes

**Pace:** ~150 words/minute, conversational, not pitch-y. Don't read it. Practice it twice, then deliver as if to a friend who's a senior engineer.

**Hit-points to land hard:**
- "It still wasn't light enough." Pause before. It's the punchline of the credibility section.
- "There was no light path." Pause after. Lets the problem statement sit.
- "Same shape as Polars, except it runs forever and serves features by key." Slow down — this is the entire product in one sentence.
- "I want them to go to Beava." Look directly at the camera here.

**Don't:**
- No jazz hands. Don't wave at the camera.
- Don't smile through the problem statements — you're describing real frustration.
- Don't say "we" — you're a solo founder; "I" is correct and stronger.
- Don't list features beyond what's in the script. The temptation is to add more; resist.

---

## Filming tips

- Plain wall behind you, daylight from the side. Not a virtual background.
- Phone camera at eye level, not below.
- Three takes. Pick the second or third — first is always stiff.
- Cut nothing in post. Continuous take signals confidence; cuts signal you're hiding something.

---

## If it runs over 60 seconds

Drop "the YC-backed real-time feature store Databricks acquired last year" → "the real-time feature store Databricks acquired." Saves ~3 seconds.

If you need ~5 more seconds, also drop "the lightest option I knew" → "Quix Streams. It still wasn't light enough."
