## Notes

- **Upsert depth scaling:** roughly linear from depth_1→depth_100, then super-linear at depth_1000 — likely BatchSize::SmallInput rebuild cost dominates the iter at depth=1000 (the `iter_batched` setup builds a 1000-version chain per iteration). Real per-write cost is closer to the depth_1→depth_100 trajectory; the depth_1000 row is conservative.
- **Lookup depth_10 < depth_1:** depth_1 timing absorbs the BTreeMap empty-tree probe overhead noise; depth_10 measurement is more representative of warm-cache cost.
- **Lookup at depth_1000 ≈ 8 µs:** BTreeMap range walk dominates. This is below the Phase 13 P99 batch-get target of 10 ms by a comfortable margin (1250×).
