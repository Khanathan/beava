<objective>
Cut over the READ paths — `GET /get/{feature}/{key}`, `POST /get`, temporal `GET /table?as_of=`, any other query — from `state_tables.lock()` to the `ApplyActor`. After this plan, the word "lock" does not appear on any production-path state access in the beava-server crate (excepting orthogonal uses: `WalSink` internals, `IdemCache` RwLock, `Registry` RwLock).

Purpose: completes the Mutex-removal story started in Plan 02. With reads also routed through the actor, we have linearizable get-after-push for free (the actor processes commands in-order), matching today's behavior without the Mutex.

Trade-off accepted (CONTEXT D-11): reads pay ~200 ns oneshot-roundtrip per query. Acceptable because `/get` latency is not the bottleneck. A future phase can introduce `ArcSwap` snapshot for lock-free reads if needed.

Output: feature_query.rs + temporal-read handlers route through the handle. Workspace tests green. Plan 04 will bench and ledger the result.
</objective>
