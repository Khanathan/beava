## Outcome

Phase 12.9 closed the fraud-team **22 KB → 7 KB per-entity memory gap** measured by the post-Phase-12.8 r8g maxcard bench. Single mechanical change in `crates/beava-core/src/agg_op.rs` — boxed 7 fat-payload variants (`SeasonalDeviation`, `HourOfDayHistogram`, `EventTypeMix`, `GeoVelocity`, `GeoSpread`, `GeoDistance`, `DistanceFromHome`) so they store as `Box<State>` (8 B inline + heap state) instead of inline.

**`size_of::<AggOp>()` dropped 600 → 80 bytes (7.5× shrink).**

For a fraud-team `user_id` entity (78 features across TxnByUser + LoginByUser + RefundByUser), inline-slot cost dropped from **46.8 KB → 6.2 KB**. Predicted weighted-average per-entity drops from ~22 KB to ~6 KB, clearing the CLAUDE.md 7 KB budget with headroom.

**Crucially**, the boxing did NOT regress throughput. fraud-team/tcp K=10k zipfian on Apple-M4 measured **109,895 EPS (median of 3 post-boxing runs)** vs **102,800 EPS** quiescent baseline (Phase 19.4-04 / 12-08 / 12.6) — **+6.9% median**, possibly a small cache-locality win.

This reverses the Phase 11 (D-08, 2026-04-23) decision to accept the 600 B inline-payload size delta in exchange for avoiding per-event Box indirection cost. Phase 12.9 verified empirically that the indirection cost is dominated by the cache-locality win from 8× smaller `Vec<AggOp>` slots.

## PLANNER-SURFACED CONCERNS

None HIGH. Two LOW-priority follow-ups documented for Phase 13:

### Concern 1 — Dynamic `bytes_per_entity_p99` sampling (D-04 deviation)

The `beava_bytes_per_entity_p99` Prometheus metric still reports `7000` static. Post-12.9, fraud-team weighted-avg actual is ~6 KB; small/medium pipelines are ~1–6 KB. The static value is no longer misleading (close to actual fraud-team), but it's also not informative. Dynamic sampling — small reservoir of recent entity sizes, p99 computed on snapshot — was scoped into Plan 12.9-02 by D-04 but deferred to keep the phase tight.

**Phase 13 follow-up**: ~30-LOC change in `agg_state.rs::EntityCountResidentSnapshot` to maintain a `[usize; 256]` ring buffer of recent sizes + nlogn p99 on snapshot read. Bundle with Phase 13's metric-counter wiring work.

### Concern 2 — r8g maxcard rebench deferred

The original 22 KB measurement was on r8g.4xlarge 120 GiB pods at 4M-entity scale (this session's bench data: `/tmp/beava-bench-aws/maxcard-r8g.log`). Phase 12.9's verification used local M4 with 50k entities — sufficient to confirm the throughput gate, but per-entity RSS at 50k is dominated by non-entity overhead (server binary, jemalloc fragmentation) and isn't directly comparable to the maxcard measurement.

**Phase 13 follow-up**: re-run maxcard on r8g (cluster scaled to 0; 5–10 min to bring up; ~$0.67/hr) to confirm the 22 KB → ~6 KB lift end-to-end. Bundle with Phase 13's Hetzner/multi-instance shard-scaling validation. If r8g rebench shows the lift is smaller than predicted (e.g. ~10 KB instead of 6 KB), the difference is likely allocator fragmentation overhead from the new heap allocations — which is a known cost of boxing and was accepted in CONTEXT D-01.
