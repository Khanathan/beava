## Decisions Made

1. **Use `cargo clippy + grep` instead of `cargo-deadcode` / `cargo-machete` / `cargo-udeps`.** None of those scanners are installed on this dev box, and Plan 13's verification needs to be reproducible from any maintainer's machine without prerequisite tool installs. `cargo clippy` is guaranteed-present and `grep` is universal. The plan's executor-instruction fallback clause explicitly authorizes this substitution.
2. **Per-bucket grep ceilings instead of a single workspace-wide percentage.** The CONTEXT discretion clause says "Planner picks `cargo-deadcode` % target during plan-phase based on baseline scan." A single percentage would mask the 95% cut in Plan 06 vs the 0% cut in Plan 10's enforcement role. Six independent ceilings with per-bucket justification gives Plan 13 mechanical, granular pass/fail signal.
3. **Plan 10 ceiling 64 → 70 (slack +9%) instead of a deletion target.** Plan 10 is mio-only architectural enforcement, not a delete-this-symbol bucket. The +6 slack covers the legitimate `agg_apply.rs` definition site, beava-core test files, criterion benches, and `recovery.rs::replay` which is allowed.
4. **Plan 06 gets the deepest cut (588 → 30, ~95%) because D-03 hard-rip removes the wire schema in entirety** — no parse-and-strip, no deprecation window. Residuals are 30 negative-path tests + register-time validator arms that REJECT pre-pivot fields.
5. **5-warning compiler-warning slack only at wave-end commits.** Any plan that finishes its wave with > 5 net new warnings re-opens. This prevents transient impl-noise from accumulating across the 13-plan sequence.
