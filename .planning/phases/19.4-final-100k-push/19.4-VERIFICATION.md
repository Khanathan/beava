**Plan 04 trace-floor miss honest accounting:** Plan 04's predicted -1,200 ns lift was based on `19.3-COST-MODEL.md §4`'s assumption that each per-desc ExtractedFields rebuild cost ~500 ns. After Plan 19.4-02 widened the SmallVec inline cap to 16, each rebuild became inline-only at ~50 ns (10× cheaper than the cost model predicted). So the realized savings on agg-stage trace was ~10% of predicted. The 100k EPS PASS gate WAS HIT (102,800 ≥ 100,000) because the hoist's effect compounds with second-order benefits (cache locality, branch prediction, stack buffer reuse) that aren't captured in the per-event ns trace timing but ARE visible in steady-state EPS.

**Process lesson (cost-model drift across sequential plans):** Plan 04's predicted 75% floor was derived from the initial 19.3 cost model BEFORE Plans 02-03 landed. Once Plan 02 widened SmallVec inline cap, the underlying scaffolding cost dropped from ~500 ns/rebuild to ~50 ns/rebuild — but the floor was not re-derived. Future phases that ship multiple sequential plans (with Plan N+1 depending on Plan N's optimizations) should re-derive cost models BEFORE each plan's floor is set, not rely on the initial pre-phase cost model. This is consistent with Phase 19.3's similar lesson where the initial cost model overestimated by 60% (memory `feedback_cost_model_from_flamegraph`).
