### Floor 3: Criterion `apply_path/warm_key/14_aggs_windowed` ≤ 295 ns

**Measured:** 408 ns post-19.4-01 (with new fixture).
**Pre-19.4 baseline (same fixture):** 434 ns.
**Floor:** 295 ns (75% of 200 ns target).
**Gap:** 113 ns over floor.

This floor was structurally unreachable on the synthetic 14-feature bench. The 14 features include 2 CountDistinct features (out of 14), so even if CountDistinct's per-call cost dropped to zero (best case), only ~14% of total apply time could be eliminated. Achieving 200 ns means dropping >50% of total apply cost — which would require optimizations to most non-CountDistinct AggKinds, which is not what Plan 19.4-01 does.

The floor was set by analogy to the fraud-team workload's 9 windowed CountDistinct features (out of ~88 = ~10% by feature count, but 9/9 are in HashSet mode at K=10k cardinality so they account for ~50% of apply CPU per the flamegraph). The synthetic 14-feature bench has 2/14 = 14% by count but 2/2 in HashSet mode. The PROPORTIONAL lift expected on the bench was 1180 × (2 × 50 / 9 × 100) ≈ ~13% of the fraud-team lift = ~150 ns/event. The measured -26 ns is ~17% of that proportional prediction — consistent with the broader observation that the FLAMEGRAPH-derived per-call cost (~118 ns/call) overestimates the actual savings (~25 ns/call).

## Why the actual lift is ~20% of predicted

Hypotheses (most-to-least likely):

1. **The flamegraph attribution conflated insertion cost with total apply cost.** `19.3-FLAMEGRAPH.md §2 row #3` reports `hashbrown::map::HashMap::insert` self-time = 9.36% of apply CPU = 1,234 ns/event. But the `insert` self-time includes work that survives the hasher swap: SIMD probe-load, slot-fill check, capacity-headroom check, eviction-tombstone scan, the `Hash::hash` outer dispatch (a function call to `hash<H: Hasher>`), and PartialEq comparison against any matched candidate slot. Only the `<u64 as Hash>::hash` body — which is a single `state.write_u64(self)` call — was replaceable. The actual SipHash `write_u64` body inside std's `DefaultHasher` is ~10-30 ns; replacing it with our identity-hasher's stored-state write is ~2 ns. So the **per-call lift is ~10-25 ns**, matching the live-trace observation (CountDistinct 457.5 → 432.1 ns/call = -25 ns/call). The `1,234 ns/event` figure was the cost of the WHOLE `HashMap::insert` family, not the SipHash hashing-of-the-key sub-step.

2. **FxHasher in `hash_value_for_hll` already runs first** (per Phase 19.2 D-02b), and that FxHash dominates the input-hashing chain. The "second hash" (SipHash inside std HashSet) is only one of two hashes; eliminating it saves at most half. The flamegraph attribution may have collapsed both hashes into the `HashMap::insert` self-time.

3. **Hashbrown's SIMD probing is not free.** The 408 ns post-19.4 cost still includes hashbrown's vectorized 16-slot probe-load + match-fingerprint check, which std HashSet (also hashbrown under the hood since Rust 1.36) was already paying. Identity hashing produces clustering that hashbrown's tombstone-scan resolves without panic but with measurable probe-extension cost — the net delta over std HashSet is smaller than the SipHash-elimination delta would suggest.

4. **Cache effects from the 64-entry HashSet warm state.** Both std and hashbrown HashSet at 64 entries fit in L1d; both should have the same memory-traffic profile. This hypothesis predicts equal cost between the two — but we DID observe -25 ns/call, so this is not a complete explanation. It bounds how big the lift could possibly be.

These hypotheses are NOT individually tested; the right diagnostic is **a focused samply re-run on post-19.4 binary** to verify the predicted hot-function shifts (per CONTEXT D-04: "spawn investigator agent (samply + per-AggKind drill-down) on post-19.4-D binary").

## What the orchestrator should surface to the user

Three options per the plan's HALT-GATE language. Recommendation: **Option 2 (re-derive thresholds)**.

### Option 1: Investigate before continuing

Spawn a debugger / samply re-run on the post-19.4-01 binary to characterize WHY CountDistinct ns/call only dropped -25 instead of -118. Specifically:
- Re-run samply at 1500 Hz on `target/release/beava-bench-v18` with the 200k events fraud-team K=10k zipfian workload.
- Verify the new top-10 hot functions: did `hashbrown::map::HashMap::insert` self-time drop from 9.36% to ~4-5% (consistent with -25 ns/call × 10 calls/event = -250 ns/event ÷ 13,180 ns/event = -1.9% of apply CPU)?

## Recommendation

**Option 2 — accept and re-derive.** Justification:

1. The optimization is correct, simple (~10 LOC), and produces a measurable +7.6% EPS lift in production.
2. The criterion floor was structurally unreachable on the 14-feature synthetic bench; the EPS+agg-stage floors were missed by single-digit percentages within typical noise.
3. The flamegraph-prediction-vs-actual ratio of ~73% on the canonical instrument suggests the predictor is roughly calibrated; the failure is a fence-post issue (the floor was set assuming the predictor was a lower bound, but it was actually a point estimate with ±25% noise).
4. Reverting forfeits real work; investigating further before 19.4-B is high-cost and likely re-confirms the same conclusion (the residual savings are unlikely to be unlockable by a different in-CountDistinct lever on the same workload).

If the orchestrator surfaces Option 2 and the user approves, the next action is to update `.planning/phases/19.4-final-100k-push/19.4-CONTEXT.md` D-03 floor table with the revised numbers and proceed to spawn Plan 19.4-02.
