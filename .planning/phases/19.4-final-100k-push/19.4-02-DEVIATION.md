## Why the floors missed

**System load variance dominates the macro signal.** Plan 01 was measured during a quiet system (Plan 01 EPS spread: 78,447–81,188, 2,741 EPS range across 3 runs). Plan 02 was measured during a loaded system (`uptime` 4.5–7.7 over the run window; concurrent Claude processes + a python http.server). Plan 02 EPS spread: 51,643–93,323, 41,680 EPS range across 22 runs (15× wider variance band).

The 9-run outer-trace agg-stage measurement spans 8,521–13,954 ns (5,433 ns range vs the predicted -530 ns lift). The signal is buried 10× below the noise floor on this hardware/load combination. The mean across all 9 runs (-448 ns) clears the floor; the median (-40 ns) misses; a single quiet run (run 6/7 at 8,521–8,645 ns) suggests -3,000 ns lift; a single loaded run (run 9 at 13,954 ns) suggests +2,287 ns regression.

**Criterion bench is structurally insensitive (Plan 01 redux).** The bench fixture (`build_fraud_team_synthetic_row_varied`) constructs 7-field rows. At cap=8 the row already fit inline (7 < 8); at cap=16 it also fits inline. The optimization-target condition (length > inline-cap → spill) is never engaged on this bench. The microbench's +5% regression on the windowed cell reflects bimodal stack-allocation noise on the larger 16-slot inline buffer (mean 406.6 → 529.3 ns; median 404.85 → 425.40 ns), not a genuine regression on the apply hot path.

**Same-pattern as Plan 19.4-01 measurement HALT.** Plan 01's lever (CountDistinct identity hasher) was structurally invisible to the 14-feature criterion bench (only 2 features hit HashSet mode, fixture corrected mid-plan). Plan 02's lever (cap-8→16 widening) is even more strongly invisible to the criterion bench (zero features even reach length 8 on this bench). The fraud-team.json TxnByUser cluster (10 fields) is the actual target; live-trace + EPS on fraud-team are the only valid measurements — and both are buried in system-load variance today.

## Disposition options (orchestrator must surface to user)

### Option 1 — RECOMMENDED: Re-measure on a quiet system

Wait for system load to drop (`uptime` < 1.5; no concurrent http server, no concurrent Claude processes, no other heavy bash sessions). Or reboot to clear caches and start cold. Re-run:

1. Outer-only trace (5 runs, expect tight ±200 ns variance band as Plan 01 had)
2. EPS suite (3 runs at 1M events, expect tight ±2,000 EPS variance band)

**Expected outcome:** Either the floors clearly clear (the structural fix lifts the apply path -397+ ns and EPS to ≥88k) or they clearly miss in a non-noise-dominated way. If the latter, Option 2 or Option 3 then applies.

**Cost:** ~5–10 minutes of bench time after system quiets. ~5 commits stay in place; only the MEASUREMENT.md verdict and SUMMARY.md need a top-up.

### Option 2 — Accept and re-derive thresholds (Plan 19.4-01 pattern)

Mirror Plan 01's accepted-with-re-derived-floor disposition. Reasoning:

- The structural correctness invariant (spill-counter test passes; cap=16 holds fraud-team's 12-field union) is achieved.
- The cap-8→16 widening is a one-line low-risk change; reverting it would re-introduce the spill on the TxnByUser cluster.
- The mean-basis agg-stage measurement (-448 ns) clears the floor; only the median basis misses (-40 ns), and the variance band makes both readings noise-dominated.
- The amortized cluster-distribution analysis (the cap-spill only triggers on the 1/5 of events hitting the TxnByUser cluster) suggests the predicted -530 ns/event was over-attributed; a more realistic ~-100 ns/event amortized prediction would not require the strict 75% floor.

Re-derive floors for Plan 03/04 using the observed prediction-realization rate from Plans 01 + 02 (Plan 01 realized ~73% of agg-stage prediction; Plan 02 realized either ~84% mean-basis or ~8% median-basis depending on read). Update CONTEXT D-03 floors before Plan 03 begins.

**Cost:** Plan 02 closes as PASS-WITH-DEFICIT; Plan 03 begins with re-derived floors.

### Option 3 — Revert and re-scope

Revert `c47c792` (the cap-16 change), leaving `ExtractedFields<'a>` at cap=8. Keep the 2 RED tests as future contracts (gated behind `#[ignore]` or removed pending re-introduction) — the cap-8 invariant means the tests would re-RED.

Reasoning to consider this only if a follow-up flamegraph proves the cap-16 change introduced a measurable cache-pressure regression in non-loaded conditions (which Option 1's quiet re-measurement would establish or refute).

**Cost:** Loses the structural fix; Plan 03/04 still need to lift Phase 19 EPS to 100k by other levers (geo pre-extraction + ExtractedFields hoist alone are predicted at -1,560 ns combined, ≥10k EPS lift, would still need to be measured against a quieter system).

## Recommendation

**Option 1.** The current measurement is dominated by system-load noise (10× wider variance than Plan 01's measurement); we cannot distinguish genuine regression from noise in the present data. A quiet re-measurement (15 minutes of bench time) will resolve the question cleanly. The structural correctness fix has landed and is verified by the spill-counter tests (RED→GREEN); the only open question is whether the macro signal clears the strict 75% floor.

If after a quiet re-measurement the floors still miss, **Option 2** is the fallback (accept and re-derive), mirroring Plan 19.4-01's accepted-with-deficit disposition. **Option 3** (revert) is only warranted if quiet re-measurement reveals a clear cache-pressure regression — unlikely given the 8 × 16 = 128-byte stack-frame growth is well below typical L1d cache-line eviction thresholds.
