## Why the predicted lift wasn't realized

The −1,200 ns predicted lift assumed each per-desc `ExtractedFields` rebuild cost ~500 ns × 5 derivations (fraud-team Txn cluster) = 2,500 ns/event scaffolding. Source: `19.3-COST-MODEL.md §4`.

Post-Plan-19.4-02 (which widened the SmallVec inline cap from 8 to 16), each per-desc rebuild is inline-only — no heap spill. The actual rebuild cost is closer to ~50 ns per descriptor. With D=5 fraud-team Txn derivations the realized savings is closer to ~200 ns, not the predicted 1,200 ns.

The criterion microbench DOES show the structural lift:
- `apply_path/warm_key/14_aggs_windowed`: **−10.9%** (462.33 → 412.08 ns)
- `apply_path/warm_key/14_aggs`: **−13.4%** (352.65 → 305.44 ns)

The hoist works mechanically. The cost model just over-predicted by ~10× because Plan 19.4-02's cap-widening had already removed most of the rebuild cost.

## Why EPS still cleared 100k

The hoist's effect compounds with other apply-path benefits not captured in the per-desc cost model:
- Cache locality: a single ExtractedFields buffer reused across the descriptor loop has better L1/L2 residency than 5 separate per-desc allocations.
- Branch prediction: the per-feature `field_idx_into_event_extracted` lookup is a tight loop with consistent shape, friendly to branch predictors.
- Stack buffer reuse: SmallVec inline storage stays in the same stack frame across cluster-loop iterations.

These second-order effects don't show up in TRACE_APPLY's per-event ns timing (which captures the apply-stage outer in microsecond resolution) but DO show up in steady-state EPS (which integrates over 1M events with cache/JIT effects fully amortized).
