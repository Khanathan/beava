| Gate | Threshold | Measured | PASS? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Agg-stage outer drop ≥ 885 ns (75% floor) | ≥ 885 ns drop | -866 ns (mean) | **NO** (-19 ns short, ~2% below floor) |

| Criterion windowed cost ≤ 295 ns (75% floor of 200 ns target) | ≤ 295 ns | 408 ns | **NO** (+113 ns over floor; bench structurally insensitive) |
| No regression on Count, Percentile, TopK ns/call > 10% | ≤ +10% on each | -5 to -10% on all (inner trace cross-band noise) | YES |

**OVERALL: HALT**

All three primary floors miss; criterion miss is structural, EPS+agg-stage miss by ~2-3% (within typical run-to-run variance, but the canonical-instrumentation mean is below the strict floor). The optimization IS landing real work (the apples-to-apples bench shows -26 ns/event, the live trace shows -25 ns CountDistinct/call, the 1M EPS rose +7.6%) but the FLAMEGRAPH-derived prediction of -1,180 ns/event was wrong by a factor of ~2× — the actual single-call cost reduction is ~25 ns rather than ~118 ns.

Per CONTEXT D-03 HALT-GATE: write `19.4-01-DEVIATION.md`, do NOT proceed to Plan 19.4-02 — the orchestrator must surface this to the user with three options:

1. **Investigate** — spawn a debugger/flamegrapher to understand WHY CountDistinct ns/call only dropped -25 (vs -118 predicted). Hypotheses in DEVIATION.md include: (a) hashbrown SIMD probing has overhead identity-hashing didn't eliminate, (b) FxHasher cost still dominates the call (NoOpHasher only saves the second-hash, not the first), (c) the flamegraph attribution to "CountDistinct probing" includes work that ISN'T removable by a hasher swap (e.g., the `Hash::hash` outer dispatch).
2. **Accept measured lift and revise downstream plan thresholds** — re-derive the 19.4-B/C/D predicted lifts using the same flamegraph-vs-actual ratio (~25 / 118 ≈ 20% realization rate); update CONTEXT D-03 floors; and proceed if the cumulative re-derived target still flips Phase 19 from PASS-WITH-DEFICIT to PASS.
3. **Skip this sub-goal and reassess Phase 19.4 scope** — revert the GREEN commit, leave count_distinct.rs at std::collections::HashSet, and reconsider whether 19.4-B/C/D alone (estimated -2,090 ns/event combined predicted lift) can hit 100k EPS without 19.4-A.

The strong recommendation is **option 2** because the measurable evidence (live trace per-AggKind, end-to-end EPS, microbench) all agree the optimization works in the right direction, and the work is one-file/~10-LOC — reverting it would forfeit the +5,624 EPS lift even though it's below the predicted floor.
