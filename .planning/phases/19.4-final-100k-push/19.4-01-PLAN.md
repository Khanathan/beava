`hashbrown::HashSet` accepts `BuildHasherDefault<H>` where `H: Hasher + Default`. The hashbrown 0.14+ docs confirm that `Hasher::write_u64` is invoked when the Eq+Hash impl on `u64` calls `state.write_u64(*self)` — verified in `core::hash::impl_write` for primitive integers. An identity hasher therefore receives exactly one `write_u64` call per insert/lookup; `write` (slice) is unreachable for u64-keyed sets and should panic via `unreachable!()`.
