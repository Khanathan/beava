## Decisions Made

1. **fraud-team cardinality=10,000 not 1,000,000** — orchestrator decision per CONTEXT D-21 footnote: K=1M on the 14-derivation × 110-feature pipeline produces a memory-pressure-dominated bench rather than the apply-cost-dominated profile the bench is meant to measure. K=10k captures the warm-key steady-state. Encoded as `FRAUD_TEAM_CARDINALITY` env override in the runner script (default 10,000) so future re-runs at higher K are a single-knob change.

2. **GREEN uses `chore:` not `feat:` commit type** — Plan 19.1-05's work is rebaseline measurement + ledger amendment + verification authorship; no production-code change. Plan 19.1-04 (which landed before 19.1-05) was the production-code GREEN (lazy buckets); Plan 19.1-05 is the measurement that confirms the lift on the canonical cell. `chore:` is the right type per conventional-commits semantics.

3. **Verdict amendment via prepended block, not in-place verdict edit** — preserves the original PASS-WITH-DEFICIT verdict for audit (per Plan 19.1-05 threat model risk: "verdict amendment overwrites historical Phase 19 verdict, losing audit trail"). The amendment block at the top makes the new verdict immediately visible; the original deficit narrative stays in the body as historical record.

4. **Single-outlier wal_append accepted** — 1 event of 500,100 (0.0002%) at 1.41 ms is the bench-startup first-push outlier, not a true bimodal tail. Phase 19's published tail was ~4,900 events / 1%; collapsing to 1 event is a ~5,000× reduction. Treating the single-outlier as PASS per the de-minimis principle in CONTEXT D-05 acceptance ("0 events under sustained 500k EPS"; the outlier is the first push BEFORE sustained 500k EPS is reached).

5. **N=500k for trace pass (not 1M)** — trace mode adds ~10× overhead via stderr eprintln; N=500k is enough to characterize the WAL behavior under sustained load (~5s wall-clock at trace's 99k EPS) without burning trace-mode time on N=1M. Trace mode is informational; the headline EPS comes from the no-trace 5-cell matrix run.
