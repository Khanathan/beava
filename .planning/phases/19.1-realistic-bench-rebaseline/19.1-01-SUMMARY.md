## Decisions Made

- **`tokio::sync::watch` (broadcast) over per-task `oneshot` or shared `AtomicBool`.** The watch channel lets multiple samplers subscribe to the same stop signal; main task does a fire-and-forget `send(())`; receivers `.changed().await` cancellation-safe inside `select!`. Cleaner than threading individual oneshots.
- **`biased;` in `select!`.** Without `biased;`, `select!` randomly polls branches — could starve the stop branch under load. With `biased;`, stop is checked first every poll, guaranteeing prompt shutdown.
- **Source-text test over behavioral timing test.** A behavioral test asserting `wall_clock_ms < 1000` for a real bench run would be flaky in CI (depends on host noise, parallelism settings, etc.). The source-text test is deterministic and cheap — `rfind` + byte-offset comparison runs in <1 ms regardless of host.
- **Keep `elapsed` capture before awaits as defense-in-depth.** Even though `tokio::select!` makes the bg-task awaits fast (∼microseconds, not seconds), pinning the order means a future refactor that removes `select!` doesn't silently re-introduce the bug — the test fails immediately.
