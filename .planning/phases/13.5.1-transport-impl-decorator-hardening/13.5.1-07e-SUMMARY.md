# Phase 13.5.1 Plan 07e: Deviation formalization cluster (Deviations 1, 2, 6) — Summary

Refactor `@bv.event` / `@bv.table` upstream-class resolver from Plan 05's 8-frame magic walk to a documented declaration-site contract (file-identity boundary detection + closure-cells direct inspection); register `atexit` cleanup for `_embed.py` per-spawn tmpdirs; cross-reference the `BEAVA_TEST_MODE` wire-through fix into the 13.4.1 closure trail; queue Deviations 4 + 5 for v0.0.x cleanup.

## Phase Goal

Plan 13.5.1-07 triaged 6 architectural deviations from Plan 05 GREEN-WITH-DEFICIT. Three earned **FORMALIZE-V0** verdicts:

- **Deviation 1** — `_resolve_upstream_proxies` walked up to 8 caller frames to support pytest-fixture pattern. Fragile to decorator-stack wrappers (lru_cache, dataclass-stacks) that shift frame depth.
- **Deviation 2** — `_embed.py` per-spawn unique tmpdir (`$TMPDIR/beava-embed-<pid>-<unix-ms>-<hex>/`) had no cleanup hook → long-lived test processes leaked disk over time.
- **Deviation 6** — `crates/beava-server/src/server.rs::build_runtime_state` honors `BEAVA_TEST_MODE=1` (Plan 13.4 D-03 wire-through gap closed by Plan 05). 13.4.1's existing SUMMARYs didn't mention it; closure trail had a hole.

This plan formalizes all three plus appends Deviations 4 + 5 (DOCUMENT-V0.0.X) to the v0.0.x cleanup queue.

## Migration Diff

### `python/beava/_table.py`

- **NEW helpers:** `_collect_closure_cells(fn)` and `_collect_caller_frame_locals()` extracted from the inline frame-walk.
- **`_collect_closure_cells`:** pairs `fn.__code__.co_freevars` with `fn.__closure__` cells — captures inner-class / lru_cache factory patterns directly, no frame walk needed.
- **`_collect_caller_frame_locals`:** walks back through frames, merges `f_locals` from every frame whose `co_filename` is NOT `_table.py`. First-seen wins (closer user-code frames override outer ones). Depth-bounded to 32 frames.
- **`_resolve_upstream_proxies`:** docstring rewritten to cite the documented contract (`docs/sdk-api/python.md` § "Supported `@bv.event` declaration sites"). Implementation: `localns = caller_locals + closure_cells` (closure cells higher priority); `get_type_hints(fn, globalns=fn.__globals__, localns=localns)`; string-fallback walks the same priority order: globals → closure cells → caller f_locals.
- **Removed:** the magic `for _ in range(8)` walk loop. The new resolver is robust to decorator-stack wrappers because the boundary is by file identity, not depth count.

## Deviations from Plan

**One mid-execution deviation worth noting (Rule 3 — refining the documented contract during implementation):**

The plan's `<declaration_site_contract>` block initially specified "single-frame caller `f_locals` (one frame back, bounded)". During Sub-task A.green implementation, the inner-class + lru_cache test cases revealed that user-code frames can be MULTIPLE frames deep when an enclosing factory fn defines the upstream class. The contract was widened mid-execution to: "walk back through user-code frames (file-identity boundary), merge `f_locals` first-seen-wins, depth-bounded to 32 frames". This widening is reflected in the final docstring + `docs/sdk-api/python.md` priority-3 row + this SUMMARY's `decisions:` frontmatter. The substantive contract guarantee — "boundary detection by file identity, not by depth count" — is unchanged; the only refinement was that the walk merges across user-code frames (per the inner-class pattern) rather than stopping at the first user-code frame.

Otherwise: plan executed exactly as written.
