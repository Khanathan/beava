## What we learned

Three structural cost-model errors documented in `19.3-COST-MODEL.md`:

1. **Wrong windowed call count.** `19.2-INVESTIGATION.md §1` said "60/88 windowed". Actual: 43/88 (verified by parsing fraud-team.json). All "60 × X ns" arithmetic was 1.4× over at the source.
2. **Per-call cost overestimated by 2-3×.** Investigation conjectured ~65 ns/call savings from the windowed dispatch elimination; actual measured savings ~30 ns/call (much of it absorbed by the new method's own Row::new() + 9-arg re-dispatch overhead).
3. **Criterion bench `14_aggs_windowed` is structurally insensitive to dispatch tax.** All 14 features fit in one warm bucket; inner state's `update_pre` short-circuits row.get for many Tier-1 kinds. The criterion never paid the ~370 ns/call windowed-inner-state cost the investigation attributed to dispatch tax.

Memory `feedback_cost_model_from_flamegraph` saved 2026-04-28 to encode the lesson: cost-model predictions must come from flamegraph + observed counters on real workload, not `per_call_ns × call_count` arithmetic.

## What changed for downstream phases

- **`19.3-FLAMEGRAPH.md`** identified 5 NEW optimization levers the original investigation missed:
  - #1 CountDistinct uses `std::HashSet<u64>` which SipHash-rehashes already-hashed u64 keys — replace with hashbrown + identity hasher (-1,180 ns/event, ~3h work)
  - #2 ExtractedFields SmallVec inline cap is 8 but TxnByUser cluster needs 10 — widen to 16 (-530 ns/event, 1-line)
  - #3 Geo `read_lat_lon` does linear `row.get(lat)` — D-01 protocol missed (-360 ns/event, ~4h)
  - #4 TopK Exact-mode BTreeMap (analogous to Phase 19.2 UDDSketch fix) (-500 ns/event, deferred to 19.5)
  - #5 EventTypeMix double HashMap lookup (-150 ns/event, deferred to 19.5)
- **Phase 19.4** adopts levers #1, #2, #3 + the carried-forward 19.3-D ExtractedFields hoist to close the 100k EPS PASS gate.
- **Phase 19.5+** picks up TopK/EventTypeMix polish + sharding deployment story if needed.

## Sequential measurement gates worked

The D-02 sequential landing decision (`19.3-CONTEXT.md`) was specifically designed to catch cost-model failures before sinking effort into downstream plans. Plan 19.3-02's measurement gate tripped after 19.3-A landed; we paused, ran flamegraph + cost-model investigation, identified the better levers, and pivoted to Phase 19.4 — exactly the design intent. Net loss: ~3 hours of investigator agent time. Counterfactual without gates: 1-2 weeks executing Plans 03/04/05 against a wrong cost model, all hitting 75% floor failures with no clear pivot path. **The gates paid for themselves.**
