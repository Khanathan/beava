## What didn't work

### Performance

- Predicted lift `60 × 65 ns = -3,900 ns/event` overestimated by ~60%; actual measured -1,526 ns (within noise).
- Criterion `14_aggs_windowed` improvement only 1.10× (target 4×).
- fraud-team EPS only +4.4% (target +27%).

### Cost-model methodology

- `19.2-INVESTIGATION.md §4` predicted-lift table used `per_call_ns × call_count_per_event` arithmetic on numbers from a microbench, never validated against a real-workload flamegraph. This is the same anti-pattern as Phase 19.2 verifier conjecture, just shifted upstream from verification to planning.
- Three errors compounded: wrong call count (60 vs 43), overestimated per-call savings (65 vs 30 ns), unaccounted optimization overhead (Row::new + 9-arg dispatch).

## Lessons recorded

1. **Memory `feedback_cost_model_from_flamegraph` (saved 2026-04-28):** Predicted lift in any future perf phase MUST come from flamegraph + observed counters on the actual target workload. Plan-checker should reject any `predicted_lift` that's `per_call_ns × call_count` arithmetic without a flamegraph reference.
2. **Sequential measurement gates between sub-goals:** Validated as a design pattern. Carry forward to Phase 19.4.
3. **Honest run-to-run variance accounting:** Phase 19.4 must report median-of-3 with ±band; do not declare wins inside the noise band.
