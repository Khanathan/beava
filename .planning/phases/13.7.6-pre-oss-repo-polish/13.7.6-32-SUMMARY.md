# Phase 13.7.6 Plan 32: Bench consolidation + `--parallel` restore

User-directive consolidation (2026-05-05 LATE): collapse the 4-binary cruft (`beava-bench`, `beava-bench-legacy`, `beava-bench-v18`, `beava-bench-v2`) into a single canonical `beava-bench` with subcommands. Reverses Plan 13.7.6-24's Option-B-strip choice — restores `--parallel` and wires it through to v18's production harness, then merges that harness into `beava-bench throughput` and deletes the v18 binary. Phase 13.8 packaging ships ONE bench binary, not four.

## Why this plan

The 4-binary split was cruft documented as a feature. Plan 26 wrote `crates/beava-bench/README.md` documenting a "decision tree" between 4 binaries — that's documenting cruft. Plan 24 (per WEBSITE-GAPS Gap 44) chose Option B (strip the lying `--parallel` flag); user direction 2026-05-05 LATE chose Option A instead — wire through, not strip — and demanded one canonical CLI. This plan delivers both halves: (a) `--parallel` restored and wired through to real parallelism, and (b) v18's 1803-LOC monolith migrated into `harness/production.rs` so a single `beava-bench` binary is the production benchmark.
