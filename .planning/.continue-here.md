| Cost-model conjecture from `per_call_ns × call_count` arithmetic | Phase 19.3-A and 19.4-A both shipped correct architectural fixes but missed predicted lift floors by 60-92% because cost models were derived from microbench arithmetic that ignored optimization's own overhead + cluster amortization + sub-step attribution | blocking | Memory `feedback_cost_model_from_flamegraph` saved. | Sub-step attribution error in flamegraph reading | Phase 19.4-A attributed full hashbrown::HashMap::insert self-time (9.36% / 1,234 ns) to SipHash; only the inner Hash::hash body (~17%) was actually replaced. SIMD probe-load, slot-fill, eviction-tombstone scan, PartialEq compare all survive a hasher swap. | advisory | When predicting lift from flamegraph: decompose self-time into sub-steps; map each to whether the optimization actually replaces it. |
| Cost-model drift across sequential plans | Phase 19.4-D predicted -1,200 ns based on cost-model assumption '5×500 ns rebuild'. Actual lift: -100 ns because Plan 19.4-02's SmallVec cap-widening had ALREADY eliminated the spill bulk by the time Plan 04 ran. | advisory | For multi-plan phases with sequential gating: re-baseline the cost model AFTER each plan lands, not just at phase start. |
| System-load contamination of perf measurement | Phase 19.4-02 measurement gate HALTed twice before passing on attempt #3. Background processes (Playwright, Arc, Cursor, Antigravity) consumed enough CPU to dominate signal — EPS spread 41,680 across 22 high-load runs vs 2,741 across 3 low-load runs (15× wider noise). | advisory | Quiet-system protocol: kill non-essential background processes before measurement; capture uptime BEFORE EACH RUN; load-filter (drop highest-2-of-7, median of 5). |
